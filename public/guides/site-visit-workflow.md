# Site Visit Workflow Guide

## Overview

The Site Visit Workflow Guide provides comprehensive instructions for field organisers conducting site visits at construction projects. This workflow combines multiple assessment activities into a streamlined process.

## Before the Site Visit

### Preparation Checklist

- Review project details and compliance history
- Confirm appointment time and site access requirements
- Check personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Download offline maps for the location
- Charge mobile device and bring backup power

### What to Bring

- CFMEU identification
- Personal protective equipment (hard hat, high-vis vest, steel-capped boots)
- Mobile device with CFMEU app installed
- Notebook and pen
- Camera (if separate from phone)
- Delegate registration forms

## Site Visit Process

### 1. Project Selection and Navigation

**Getting to the Site:**
- Use the mobile app's "Closest to Me" feature to locate nearby projects
- Verify project address and builder details
- Note travel time for future scheduling

**Site Access:**
- Sign in at site office if required
- Follow all site safety induction procedures
- Meet with site manager or delegate

### 2. Initial Site Assessment

**Visual Inspection:**
- Observe site conditions and safety measures
- Note visible union presence (posters, delegates, member stickers)
- Identify potential compliance issues
- Document workforce composition

**Documentation:**
- Take photos of site entrance and safety signage
- Record number of workers visible
- Note any subcontractor vehicles

### 3. Contact Mapping

**Key Contacts to Identify:**
- Site manager/supervisor
- Union delegates
- Health and safety representatives
- Leading hands or foremen

**Information to Collect:**
- Names and roles
- Contact details
- Union membership status
- Willingness to assist with future communications

### 4. Compliance Assessment

**Safety Standards:**
- Check amenities facilities (toilets, change rooms, meal areas)
- Verify safety signage and procedures
- Assess housekeeping standards
- Document any safety concerns

**Employment Standards:**
- Observe worker conditions and treatment
- Note any potential sham contracting arrangements
- Check for proper safety inductions
- Assess working conditions

### 5. Delegate Engagement

**Identify Potential Delegates:**
- Look for respected, experienced workers
- Note workers who demonstrate leadership qualities
- Consider union members who show interest

**Delegate Discussion Topics:**
- Role and responsibilities of delegates
- Training opportunities available
- Support provided by CFMEU
- Site-specific concerns to monitor

### 6. Workforce Documentation

**Information to Record:**
- Total number of workers on site
- Union members vs non-members
- Apprentice numbers
- Major subcontractor presence

**Examples:**
- "15 workers total, 8 union members, 3 apprentices"
- "Major subcontractors: XYZ Electrical, ABC Plumbing"
- "New group of 5 workers started this week"

### 7. Site Photography

**Required Photos:**
- Site entrance and signage
- Amenities facilities
- Safety notice boards
- Union delegate presence (with permission)
- Any compliance issues (discreetly)

**Photography Guidelines:**
- Always respect privacy and safety
- Ask permission before photographing workers
- Focus on conditions, not individuals
- Document compliance issues constructively

## Post-Site Visit Actions

### Immediate Follow-up

1. **Submit Site Visit Report**
   - Complete all required fields
   - Upload photos and documentation
   - Record compliance ratings
   - Note any immediate action required

2. **Update Project Records**
   - Add new contacts discovered
   - Update workforce numbers
   - Record compliance issues found
   - Note delegate interest or commitments

3. **Delegate Task Assignment**
   - Create specific monitoring tasks for delegates
   - Set clear deadlines and expectations
   - Provide contact information for support
   - Schedule follow-up communications

### Ongoing Monitoring

**Weekly Actions:**
- Contact delegates for updates
- Monitor compliance trends
- Address emerging issues
- Schedule return visits if needed

**Monthly Reviews:**
- Assess overall compliance trends
- Evaluate delegate effectiveness
- Plan strategic interventions
- Report to lead organiser

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Access Issues

**Problem:** Site management denies access or is uncooperative

**Solutions:**
- Explain legal rights of access for union officials
- Contact lead organiser for support
- Schedule alternative visit time
- Document access refusal for compliance reporting

### Safety Concerns

**Problem:** Serious safety risks identified during visit

**Solutions:**
- Immediately report to site management
- Document with photos and notes
- Notify WorkCover if necessary
- Follow up to ensure corrective action

### Hostile Reception

**Problem:** Workers or management are hostile to union presence

**Solutions:**
- Remain professional and calm
- Focus on safety and worker rights
- Build relationships through regular visits
- Engage friendly workers as allies

## Mobile App Integration

### Using the Site Visit Wizard

1. **Preparation**
   - Select target project from mobile dashboard
   - Review project history and compliance status
   - Download offline maps for navigation

2. **On-Site Data Entry**
   - Use mobile forms for real-time data capture
   - Take photos directly through the app
   - Record GPS coordinates for accurate location data

3. **Offline Capability**
   - Continue working without internet connection
   - Data syncs automatically when connection restored
   - All work is saved locally as backup

### Field Tips

**Battery Management:**
- Enable battery saving mode during long visits
- Carry portable power pack
- Close unnecessary apps
- Use airplane mode in poor signal areas

**Data Accuracy:**
- Double-check information before submitting
- Use photo documentation to verify claims
- Record specific details rather than general observations
- Note exact names and contact information

## Communication Strategies

### Building Rapport

**First Impressions:**
- Arrive on time and professionally dressed
- Introduce yourself clearly and state your purpose
- Listen more than you speak initially
- Show genuine interest in workers' concerns

**Ongoing Relationships:**
- Visit consistently and predictably
- Follow through on promises and commitments
- Provide value through safety information and support
- Respect workers' time and workplace priorities

### Handling Difficult Conversations

**Principles:**
- Stay calm and professional
- Focus on facts and legal requirements
- Avoid personal attacks or emotional responses
- Know when to disengage and seek support

**Techniques:**
- Use "I" statements to express concerns
- Ask open-ended questions to understand perspectives
- Acknowledge valid points raised by others
- Redirect conversations to constructive topics

## Documentation and Record Keeping

### Site Visit Reports

**Essential Elements:**
- Date, time, and location of visit
- Site contacts and their roles
- Workforce composition and union density
- Compliance assessment and issues identified
- Photos and supporting evidence
- Action items and follow-up requirements

### Best Practices

**Consistency:**
- Use standardized templates and terminology
- Complete all required fields thoroughly
- Submit reports promptly after visits
- Maintain chronological organization

**Quality:**
- Include specific details and examples
- Support observations with photos or documents
- Distinguish between facts and opinions
- Note sources of information provided

## Safety Considerations

### Personal Safety

**Required Equipment:**
- Appropriate PPE for construction sites
- Mobile phone with emergency contacts
- First aid knowledge or basic supplies
- Situational awareness training

**Risk Assessment:**
- Evaluate site conditions before entry
- Note potential hazards and control measures
- Have emergency exit plans
- Know when to leave unsafe situations

### Legal Rights and Responsibilities

**Right of Access:**
- Union officials have legal rights to enter construction sites
- Must comply with site safety requirements
- Cannot interfere with normal work operations
- Must identify themselves and state their purpose

**Reporting Obligations**
- Serious safety issues must be reported
- Illegal activities must be documented and reported
- Worker confidentiality must be maintained
- Accurate record-keeping is essential

## Conclusion

The site visit workflow is a critical component of effective union organizing. By following this structured approach, organisers can:

- Build strong relationships with workers and delegates
- Monitor and improve site compliance
- Identify and address safety issues
- Grow union membership and influence
- Document evidence for industrial action if needed

Consistent, professional site visits create a foundation for successful organizing campaigns and improved working conditions for all construction workers.
