# Employer Ratings System v2 Guide

## Overview

The Employer Ratings System v2 provides a comprehensive framework for assessing construction employers across multiple dimensions. This system helps organizers track employer behavior, identify patterns, and make informed decisions about organizing strategies.

## Rating Philosophy

### Purpose and Goals

**Primary Objectives:**
- Provide objective assessment of employer behavior
- Track compliance with industry standards
- Identify organizing opportunities and risks
- Support evidence-based industrial strategies
- Create historical record of employer performance

**Key Principles:**
- Evidence-based assessments
- Role-appropriate evaluation tracks
- Confidence-weighted ratings
- Regular updates and reviews
- Transparent rating criteria

### Rating Tracks

The system uses three distinct rating tracks to accommodate different types of knowledge and evidence:

**1. Project Data Track**
- Based on verifiable project records
- Formal documentation and reports
- Compliance history and incidents
- Payment and EBA verification

**2. Organiser Expertise Track**
- Personal experience and observations
- Site visits and direct interactions
- Relationship quality assessments
- Professional judgment and expertise

**3. Worker Feedback Track**
- Union member reports and feedback
- Delegate observations and concerns
- Worker complaints and grievances
- Anonymous member surveys

## Rating Categories

### 1. Union Respect (4-Point Assessment)

**Rating Scale:**
- **Excellent (4)**: Strong union relationships, active collaboration
- **Good (3)**: Respectful relationship, reasonable cooperation
- **Poor (2)**: Minimal cooperation, some resistance
- **Hostile (1)**: Active opposition to union activities

**Assessment Criteria:**
- Delegate treatment and recognition
- Union meeting access and facilities
- Communication responsiveness
- Industrial cooperation level
- Historical relationship patterns

**Examples:**
- **Excellent**: "Employer provides union meeting rooms, actively consults with delegates, responds promptly to communications"
- **Good**: "Employer allows reasonable union access, treats delegates professionally"
- **Poor**: "Employer restricts union access, delays responses to delegate inquiries"
- **Hostile**: "Employer actively undermines union activities, intimidates union supporters"

### 2. Safety Standards (Traffic Light System)

**Rating Scale:**
- **Green**: Exceeds safety requirements, strong safety culture
- **Amber**: Meets minimum requirements, areas for improvement
- **Red**: Serious safety deficiencies, immediate action required

**Assessment Areas:**
- Safety management systems
- Incident reporting and response
- Worker training and supervision
- Site conditions and housekeeping
- Amenities and facilities

**Specific Indicators:**
- **Green**: Regular safety audits, proactive hazard identification, worker safety committees
- **Amber**: Basic safety compliance, some delayed hazard rectification, minimal worker involvement
- **Red**: Frequent safety incidents, poor hazard reporting, inadequate worker protection

### 3. Sham Contracting Risk Assessment

**Risk Levels:**
- **Low**: Proper employee relationships, compliant arrangements
- **Medium**: Some contracting concerns, requires monitoring
- **High**: Significant sham contracting risks, investigation needed

**Warning Signs:**
- Workers required to have own ABN and insurance
- Invoice-based payment systems
- Workers providing own tools and equipment
- No employee benefits (leave, superannuation)
- Pressure to register as contractors

**Assessment Process:**
- Review worker classification arrangements
- Examine payment structures and systems
- Interview workers about employment terms
- Check for appropriate workers compensation
- Verify superannuation compliance

### 4. Wage Compliance Assessment

**Compliance Areas:**
- Award/EBA payment compliance
- Penalty rate application
- Allowance payments
- Superannuation contributions
- Pay slip accuracy and transparency

**Rating Methodology:**
- Documented underpayment issues
- Frequency and severity of breaches
- Response to wage complaints
- Historical compliance patterns
- Audit results and rectification

**Evidence Sources:**
- Worker complaints and reports
- Fair Work Ombudsman investigations
- Union audits and reviews
- Pay slip analysis
- Industry intelligence

### 5. Training Investment Assessment

**Metrics and Indicators:**
- Apprentice to tradesperson ratios
- Training program quality and completion
- Investment in skill development
- Worker progression and advancement
- Industry training participation

**Assessment Criteria:**
- Apprentice employment levels
- Training completion rates
- Investment in upskilling
- Support for formal qualifications
- Career development opportunities

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Compare to industry averages
- Track improvement over time
- Consider project-specific factors
- Evaluate commitment to workforce development

### 6. Job Security Assessment

**Security Indicators:**
- Employment stability and continuity
- Redundancy practices and support
- Work duration and predictability
- Subcontractor usage patterns
- Industry reputation

**Assessment Factors:**
- Project bidding and tendering practices
- Worker retention rates
- Handling of economic downturns
- Support for displaced workers
- Long-term employment relationships

## Confidence Scoring System

### Confidence Levels

**Very High (95%)**
- Direct personal experience with employer
- Multiple recent interactions and observations
- Verified information from reliable sources
- Comprehensive understanding of operations

**High (80%)**
- Regular contact and solid understanding
- Some direct experience and observations
- Information from multiple reliable sources
- Good knowledge of employer practices

**Medium (60%)**
- Limited direct experience or observations
- Mixed or partially verified information
- Some knowledge of employer operations
- Information from single sources

**Low (40%)**
- Minimal direct contact or experience
- Second-hand or unverified information
- Limited knowledge of current practices
- Outdated or incomplete information

### Impact on Ratings

**Weighting System:**
- Higher confidence ratings carry more weight in calculations
- Low confidence ratings require additional verification
- Confidence affects trend analysis and pattern identification
- System tracks confidence changes over time

**Verification Requirements:**
- Low confidence ratings trigger additional review
- Medium confidence ratings benefit from supplementary evidence
- High confidence ratings can be used for strategic decisions
- Very high confidence ratings can influence industrial action

## Mobile Rating Workflow

### Using the Mobile Rating Wizard

**Access and Navigation:**
- Select employer from project page or employer directory
- Tap "Rate Employer" button
- Choose appropriate rating track
- Proceed through guided rating process

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Track Selection**
   - Choose Project Data, Organiser Expertise, or Worker Feedback track
   - Review track-specific guidance
   - Confirm selection and proceed

2. **Category Assessment**
   - Rate each applicable category
   - Provide specific examples and evidence
   - Set confidence level for each rating
   - Add relevant notes and observations

3. **Evidence Documentation**
   - Upload supporting documents and photos
   - Record specific incidents or examples
   - Note dates and relevant details
   - Link to related projects or activities

4. **Review and Submit**
   - Review all ratings for consistency
   - Confirm confidence levels
   - Submit rating with comments
   - Receive confirmation and summary

## Best Practices

### Rating Guidelines

**Evidence-Based Assessment:**
- Base ratings on verifiable information
- Document specific examples and incidents
- Use multiple sources when possible
- Update ratings regularly with new information

**Consistency and Objectivity:**
- Apply rating criteria consistently
- Avoid personal bias in assessments
- Consider context and circumstances
- Separate opinions from documented facts

**Professional Standards:**
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information
- Respect worker privacy and safety
- Follow legal and ethical guidelines
- Document reasoning for ratings

### Quality Assurance

**Peer Review Process:**
- Have ratings reviewed by colleagues
- Discuss differing perspectives
- Reach consensus on controversial ratings
- Learn from experienced organizers

**Regular Updates:**
- Update ratings when new information becomes available
- Review and adjust confidence levels
- Remove outdated or irrelevant ratings
- Maintain current and accurate assessments

### Communication and Documentation

**Clear Documentation:**
- Provide detailed explanations for ratings
- Include specific examples and evidence
- Note dates and relevant circumstances
- Use professional and objective language

**Strategic Communication:**
- Share ratings with appropriate stakeholders
- Use ratings to support organizing strategies
- Communicate findings to union leadership
- Coordinate with industrial officers
